# Waters Under Pressure

A single-device water-literacy team game for the eTwinning project **Guardians of our Waters**.

**[▶ Play live](https://YOUR-USERNAME.github.io/YOUR-REPOSITORY/)** — works in any modern browser on a phone, tablet, laptop, classroom computer or shared screen. No installation, no accounts, no database, no Firebase backend.

## What it is

**Waters Under Pressure** is a team strategy game for teenagers. One shared device becomes the mission console. Teams read environmental evidence, interpret real-style water-quality data, and make decisions under pressure.

Each round presents a water-related incident, such as storm runoff, an algal bloom, plastic litter, microbial contamination, upstream/downstream nutrient pressure, or school water management. Teams must decide what is happening, which evidence matters most, what action should be taken first, and how the community or authorities should respond.

The game is designed to feel like a real game, not a worksheet:

- **2–3 teams**
- **6 rounds**
- **One shared device**
- **Automatic scoring**
- **No teacher judging**
- **No voting**
- **No subjective style points**

Designed mainly for students aged **14–19**. Typical playing time: **25–40 minutes**.

---

## Single-device mode

This version is built for **one device only**.

You can play with:

- one phone passed between teams
- one tablet on a desk
- one laptop connected to a projector
- one shared screen during a guided online lesson

All teams see the same evidence. Then each team takes the device in turn and answers privately. Teams should not watch each other’s choices before submitting.

This version does **not** synchronize multiple devices. An online multiplayer version is planned separately for teams in different cities or countries.

## How to play

### Setup

1. Open the live link, or open the HTML file in a browser.
2. Click **Create Mission**.
3. Choose **2 or 3 teams**.
4. Select team identities or enter custom team names.
5. Click **Start Mission**.
6. Read the mission briefing together.

### Each round

1. Click **Start Round**.
2. The game performs a **River Roll** and reveals the challenge type.
3. Read the **Evidence Card** together:
   - location
   - recent conditions
   - field observations
   - water-quality data
4. Click **Open Team Decisions**.
5. Each team opens its **Private Answer Panel** when it is their turn.
6. The team answers the 4 questions and clicks **Lock In Answers**.
7. After all teams submit, click **Reveal Results**.
8. Review the correct answers, explanation, and scores.
9. Continue to the next round.

There are **6 rounds total**.

---

## Questions and scoring

Each round has 4 multiple-choice questions.

| Question type | Points |
|---|---:|
| Correct diagnosis | +2 |
| Correct evidence choice | +2 |
| Correct first action | +3 |
| Correct communication / collaboration / policy choice | +1 |
| **Maximum per round** | **8** |

The game calculates all points automatically. The winner is the team with the highest **Guardian Score** after 6 rounds.

Perfect rounds, streaks, unaided badges and medals are included for fun and motivation, but the leaderboard is based on the Guardian Score.

## Hints and Verification Tokens

### Reasoning Hints

Every question has one free **Reasoning Hint**.

Hints:

- do not reveal the answer directly
- do not reduce the score
- help students read the evidence more carefully
- are useful for discussion and learning

### Verification Tokens

Each team starts with **2 Verification Tokens** for the whole mission.

A team may spend 1 token during a round to reveal one extra clue. Tokens are limited, so teams must decide when the extra evidence is worth it.

## Challenge types

The game includes six types of water-pressure missions:

| Challenge type | What students practise |
|---|---|
| **Field Test** | Choosing useful measurements and missing evidence |
| **Pollution Alert** | Identifying likely pollution sources or pressures |
| **Rapid Response** | Selecting the best immediate action |
| **Community Message** | Communicating risk responsibly |
| **Cross-Border Link** | Connecting evidence across teams or locations |
| **Policy Window** | Choosing realistic school or local authority action |

## Tips for teachers

- Use a projector if the whole class is playing together.
- Let teams discuss quietly before opening their private panel.
- When one team is answering, ask the other teams to look away.
- Encourage students to explain *why* they chose an answer after results are revealed.
- Use the explanations as short science, citizenship or environmental literacy discussions.
- Keep the pace fast. The game works best when teams feel some pressure but still have time to reason.

Suggested classroom formats:

| Class situation | Suggested setup |
|---|---|
| Small group | 2 teams around one phone or tablet |
| Whole class | 2 or 3 teams, laptop + projector |
| Computer lab | One shared game projected, teams discuss separately |
| Online lesson | Teacher shares screen and enters each team’s answers in turn |

## Project context

**Waters Under Pressure** was created for the eTwinning project **Guardians of our Waters**, connecting schools across Italy, Spain, Greece, France, Slovakia and Bulgaria.

The game supports the project’s goals:

1. Scientific inquiry and water-quality data literacy
2. International collaboration and shared environmental responsibility
3. Environmental advocacy and community engagement
4. Interdisciplinary learning across science, citizenship and digital skills
5. Geospatial and systems thinking
6. European citizenship through cooperation
